Pate

Haitian Pâtés (Pâté Haïtien) are savory pastries filled with a spiced meat mixture, commonly enjoyed as snacks or appetizers. It is still my top favorite snack, and my number one target at every haitian party I attend! As a child, I remember knitting the dough actively with my mom laughing and us ejoying this snack during our movie nights.

prep time

45 min.

cook time

25 min.

serving

10 servings

Tags : Soups Appetizers Meals Desserts

instructions

step 1

Mix the dry ingredients in a large bowl and add a mixture of water and spices of your choices until your form a firm dough. Add the cold butter and the oil, until the dough comes together and let it chill.

step 2

To make the filling, mix in a bowl the fiiling of your choice (pepper, tomatoes, onions, cheese, ham, meat...). On a floured surface, roll out the dough and cut it into circle. Add 1 tablespoon of filling and use a fork to seal the dough.

step 3

Heat oil in a pan at 380 degres and deep fry the pâtés for 10 mins in each side

ingredients

Dough: 3 cups of All purpose flour | 1 teaspoon of salt | 1/2 of water 1 cup of cold butter | 1 teaspoon of oil

Filling: Chopped peppers, onions, ground beef, parsley and other filling of choice

Large bowl

large pan

Chaka

Haitian Chaka is a hearty and nutritious dish made with cornmeal, beans, and a variety of vegetables and seasonings. Although i disliked it as a kid, it is now one of my comfort meals.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

1 hour 30 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Soups Appetizers Meals Desserts

instructions

step 1

Drain the soaked beans and place them in a large pot with 6 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the beans are tender, about 1 hour. Drain and set aside.

step 2

In the same pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, bell pepper, carrot, and pumpkin or squash. Sauté for about 5 minutes, until the vegetables are softened. Add the cornmeal to the pot and stir to combine with the vegetables. Gradually add the coconut milk, stirring constantly to prevent lumps. Cook for about 10 minutes, until the mixture thickens

step 3

Add the cooked beans, thyme, parsley, Scotch bonnet pepper (if using), salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir well to combine. Reduce heat to low and simmer for another 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the flavors meld and the chaka reaches a porridge-like consistency.

ingredients

1 cup dried red beans, soaked overnight, 1 cup of cornmeal, 2 cups of coconut milk

1 chopped inion, 2 garlic cloves, 1 bell pepper, 1 cup of pumpkin

1 teaspoon of thyme, 1 teaspoon of parsly, salt and pepper to taste, 6 cups of water, 2 tbsp of veg. oil

tools

large pot with lid

large spoon

Plantain Porridge

Haitian Plantain Porridge is a creamy, flavorful dish that's perfect for breakfast or a snack.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Soups Appetizers Meals Desserts

instructions

step 1

Place the plantain chunks and 2 cups of water in a blender. Blend until smooth.

step 2

Pour the blended plantain mixture into a large pot. Add the remaining 2 cups of water, coconut milk, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly to prevent lumps, until the mixture thickens, about 15 minutes.

step 3

Stir in the sweetened condensed milk and vanilla extract. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, stirring frequently, until the porridge is creamy and well combined. Adjust sweetness to taste. Serve hot.

ingredients

2 plantains, 4 coups of water, 1 cup of coconut milk

1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk, 1 tsp vanilla extract

1/2 tsp ground cinnamon, nutmeg, a pinch if salt

tools

Hand Blender

Large Heavy Pot With Lid and spoon

Blan Manje

Haitian Blancmange (Blan Manje) is a creamy coconut pudding often enjoyed as a dessert. It can be really sweet, which is why it's always been my dad's favorite.

prep time

5 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Soups Appetizers Meals Desserts

instructions

step 1

In a small bowl, dissolve the cornstarch in 1/2 cup of water, stirring until smooth.

step 2

In a medium saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sweetened condensed milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly. Once the mixture starts to warm up, slowly add the cornstarch mixture, continuing to stir. Cook until the mixture thickens and starts to bubble, about 10-15 minutes.

step 3

Remove from heat and pour the pudding into individual serving dishes or a large bowl. Allow it to cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours or until set. Serve chilled.

ingredients

1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk, 1 can (14 oz) sweetened condensed milk

1/4 cup cornstarch, 1/2 cup water,1 teaspoon vanilla extract

1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon, 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg

tools

Large bowl

Spoon

Akasan

Haitian Akasan is a creamy and sweet cornmeal drink that's typically enjoyed as a breakfast or a dessert

prep time

5 min.

cook time

25 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : Soups Appetizers Meals Desserts

instructions

step 1

In a medium bowl, combine the cornmeal with 2 cups of water. Stir until the cornmeal is well-dissolved.

step 2

In a large pot, bring the remaining 2 cups of water to a boil. Gradually add the cornmeal mixture to the boiling water, stirring constantly to prevent lumps. Reduce the heat to medium-low and cook, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens, about 10 minutes.

step 3

Add the coconut milk, regular milk, sweetened condensed milk, vanilla extract, ground cinnamon, ground nutmeg, and a pinch of salt to the pot. Add the cinnamon sticks if using. Continue to cook on low heat, stirring frequently, for another 10 minutes until the akasan is creamy and well combined. Adjust sweetness to taste. Remove the cinnamon sticks before serving.

ingredients

1 cup fine yellow cornmeal, 4 cups water, 1 can (13.5 oz) coconut milk 1 cup milk

1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk, 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon, 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg, A pinch of salt, 1-2 cinnamon sticks (optional)

Chiktay

Haitian Chiktay is a flavorful shredded fish dish often enjoyed as an appetizer or snack.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

12 servings

Tags : Soups Appetizers Meals Desserts

instructions

step 1

Soak the salted codfish overnight in cold water, changing the water several times to remove excess salt. Drain and rinse the codfish. Place the codfish in a pot of fresh water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for about 10 minutes. Drain and let cool. Once cooled, shred the codfish into small pieces, removing any bones.

step 2

In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onion, bell pepper, Scotch bonnet pepper, and minced garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are soft and fragrant, about 5-7 minutes.

step 3

Add the shredded codfish to the skillet with the vegetables. Stir in the lime juice, thyme, parsley, black pepper, and ground cloves (if using). Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until everything is well combined and heated through. Adjust seasoning to taste.

ingredients

1 pound salted codfish (bacalao), 1 medium onion, thinly sliced 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced, 1-2 Scotch bonnet peppers, thinly sliced

2 cloves garlic, minced, 1/4 cup olive oil,1/4 cup fresh lime juice, 1 teaspoon thyme

1 teaspoon parsley, chopped, 1/2 teaspoon black pepper, 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves (optional)

tools

bowl,Pot With Lid

Measuring Spoons and knife

Diri Djon Djon

Haitian Diri Djon Djon, or black mushroom rice, is a flavorful and aromatic dish made with a special type of dried black mushrooms called djon djon.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

40 min.

serving

10 servings

Tags : Soups Appetizers Meals Desserts

instructions

step 1

Rinse the dried djon djon mushrooms and soak them in 4 cups of water for at least 30 minutes. After soaking, bring the mushrooms and water to a boil, then reduce the heat and let simmer for 15 minutes. Strain the liquid through a fine sieve or cheesecloth into a bowl, discarding the mushrooms. You should have about 3 to 4 cups of djon djon broth.

step 2

In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Sauté until the vegetables are soft and fragrant, about 5 minutes.

step 3

Add the rice to the pot with the sautéed vegetables and stir to coat the rice with the oil. Pour in the djon djon broth, ensuring there is about 3 to 4 cups of liquid (add water if necessary). Stir in the thyme, parsley, green peas, and diced carrots. Add the Scotch bonnet pepper if using. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed. Remove the Scotch bonnet pepper before serving.

ingredients

1 cup dried djon djon mushrooms, 4 cups water, 2 cups long-grain rice, 1 medium onion, finely chopped, 1 bell pepper, finely chopped

2 cloves minced garlic, 1 cup green peas, 1 cup diced carrots, 2 tablespoons vegetable oil

1 teaspoon thyme, 1 teaspoon parsley, chopped, 1 Scotch bonnet pepper (optional), Salt and pepper to taste

tools

Large Heavy Pot With Lid

Measuring Spoons and large spoon

Lalo

Haitian Lalo is a traditional leafy green stew often made with spinach, okra, and meat.

prep time

15 min.

cook time

1 hour 15 min.

serving

10 servings

Tags : Soups Appetizers Meals Desserts

instructions

step 1

In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the cubed meat and brown on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.

step 2

Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the pot with the browned meat. Cook until the onion is translucent, about 3-5 minutes. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and tomato paste. Cook for another 2 minutes. Add the chopped spinach and sliced okra to the pot. Pour in the water or broth. Add the whole Scotch bonnet pepper if using. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

step 3

Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let simmer, partially covered, for about 1 hour, or until the meat is tender and the vegetables are cooked through. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.

ingredients

1 pound meat of your choice (chicken, beef, or pork), cubed, 1 pound fresh spinach, chopped, 1 pound fresh okra, sliced, 1 large onion, chopped

3 cloves garlic, minced, 2 tomatoes, chopped, 2 tablespoons tomato paste, 1 Scotch bonnet pepper, whole (optional, for heat)

1/4 cup oil, 4 cups water or broth, Salt and pepper to taste

tools

Large Heavy Pot With Lid

Measuring Spoons and large spoon

Measuring Cups
